About Me

Game developer passionate about data-driven design and building great teams

I'm a game developer and team lead at Replai, where I lead a team of 10+ developers and designers working on top-10 grossing mobile games. I've been creating games since I was 14—starting with mods and maps for CS2D—and have spent the past two years focused on match-3 and probability-based game development.

I hold a Master's degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Belgrade and studied mathematics at the University of Belgrade. During my studies, I was a straight-A student, awarded the Vukovac distinction and placed in regional math competitions. I also worked as a math tutor and contributed as a translator for BoardGameArena.

I approach game design from an analytical perspective—I'm passionate about player data, retention metrics, and understanding what makes games genuinely fun. At 20, I built and monetized a multiplayer football game that sustained a 100-player community with real-money transactions.

I'm now looking for my next challenge where I can apply my technical skills and leadership experience to more complex projects with a strong team. I value data-driven design, constant iteration, and building games that players love.

Where It All Started

At 14, I started creating mods and maps for CS2D using Lua scripting. My work gained traction quickly—with creations reaching over 1,000 downloads and being hosted on dozens of servers. I became an official USGN modder and mapper, which taught me the fundamentals of game logic, player engagement, and community building.

This early experience shaped how I think about games today. I learned that great gameplay comes from iteration, listening to players, and understanding what keeps them coming back.

Beyond Digital: Board Game Design

I've designed and crafted 2 original board games, later creating digital versions for Android. This cross-medium approach strengthened my understanding of core mechanics and iterative design. Working without the crutch of digital effects forced me to focus on what truly makes a game fun at its core.

I also contributed as a translator for BoardGameArena, translating 2 games and most of the website UI. I even created a script that automatically adapted Serbian translations to Croatian, which impressed the staff and doubled our language coverage overnight.

HaxBo: Mathematical Systems Design

In 2018, I created my largest personal project: HaxBo, an enhanced modification of the popular game Haxball. This project showcases my ability to combine programming, mathematics, and game design.

Key Achievement: Designed and implemented an in-game gambling system using mathematical probability models, providing entertainment while maintaining fair odds and economic balance.

The project demonstrates proficiency in JavaScript, database management, cloud services, and community management (200+ active players). Over 5,400 lines of code managing game rules, player statistics, inventory systems, and real-time interactions.
